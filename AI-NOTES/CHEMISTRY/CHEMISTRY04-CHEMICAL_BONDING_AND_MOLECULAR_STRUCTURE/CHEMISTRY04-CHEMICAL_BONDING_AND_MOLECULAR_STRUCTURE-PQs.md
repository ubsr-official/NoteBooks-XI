**Q51.**
**Assertion (A):** The bond length in O₂ (121 pm) is shorter than that in H₂O₂ (148 pm for O−O single bond).
**Reason (R):** A higher bond order corresponds to a shorter and stronger bond.

---

**Q52.**
**Assertion (A):** CO₂ has zero dipole moment despite having polar C=O bonds.
**Reason (R):** CO₂ is a linear molecule, and the two equal and opposite bond dipoles cancel each other vectorially.

---

**Q53.**
**Assertion (A):** NH₃ has a higher dipole moment than NF₃.
**Reason (R):** In NH₃, the lone pair orbital dipole and the resultant bond dipoles act in the same direction, while in NF₃ they act in opposite directions.

---

**Q54.**
**Assertion (A):** He₂ molecule does not exist.
**Reason (R):** The bond order of He₂, calculated from MO theory, is zero.

---

**Q55.**
**Assertion (A):** The O−O bond length in O₃ (ozone) is 128 pm, which is intermediate between O−O single bond (148 pm) and O=O double bond (121 pm).
**Reason (R):** O₃ exhibits resonance, and the actual molecule is a hybrid of two canonical structures.

---

**Q56.**
**Assertion (A):** F₂ has a lower bond dissociation enthalpy than Cl₂.
**Reason (R):** Due to the smaller size of F atom, there is greater lone pair−lone pair repulsion between the two F atoms in F₂, weakening the bond.

---

**Q57.**
**Assertion (A):** PCl₅ obeys the octet rule.
**Reason (R):** PCl₅ is a molecule of the third period element P, which can expand its octet by using 3d orbitals.

---

**Q58.**
**Assertion (A):** The bond angle in H₂O (104.5°) is less than in NH₃ (107°).
**Reason (R):** Water has two lone pairs on oxygen while ammonia has only one lone pair on nitrogen. Greater lp−lp repulsion in H₂O causes greater bond compression.

---

**Q59.**
**Assertion (A):** O₂ molecule is paramagnetic.
**Reason (R):** The MO electronic configuration of O₂ contains two unpaired electrons in degenerate π*2p antibonding molecular orbitals.

---

**Q60.**
**Assertion (A):** Intramolecular hydrogen bonding in o-nitrophenol results in a lower boiling point compared to p-nitrophenol.
**Reason (R):** Intramolecular H-bonds form a closed ring within the molecule, reducing intermolecular association and thus lowering boiling point.

---

**Q61.**
**Assertion (A):** In the resonance hybrid of CO₃²⁻, all C−O bond lengths are equal.
**Reason (R):** The three canonical forms of CO₃²⁻ are equivalent, and the resonance hybrid correctly represents the actual molecule.

---

**Q62.**
**Assertion (A):** Sigma bonds allow rotation about the bond axis, but pi bonds do not.
**Reason (R):** Sigma bonds are formed by axial overlap (cylindrically symmetric), while pi bonds require parallel p orbital alignment which would be disrupted by rotation.

**Q81.** 🟡 Write the Lewis dot structures for the following and indicate all formal charges:
(i) N₂O (nitrous oxide — skeletal structure N−N−O)
(ii) SO₃

**Solution:**

**(i) N₂O:**

* Total valence electrons: N(5) + N(5) + O(6) = 16
* Skeletal: N−N−O (terminal N is central N's neighbour; N is central, O is terminal)
* Three canonical forms possible. Main structure: :N≡N−Ö: where N(left) has FC = 5−2−½(6) = −1; N(right) has FC = 5−0−½(8) = +1; O has FC = 6−4−½(2) = 0
* Resonance hybrid of :N≡N−Ö: ↔ :N=N=O: ↔ :N−N≡O:
* **Answer:** N₂O is linear; resonance hybrid; bond orders intermediate

**(ii) SO₃:**

* Total valence electrons: S(6) + 3×O(6) = 24
* Three canonical forms (S double-bonded to one O, single to two Os); all O and S equivalent in resonance hybrid
* **Resonance hybrid:** S−O bond order = 4/3 = 1.33; each O has FC = 0; S has FC = 0 in the preferred structure; molecule is trigonal planar

**Q83.** 🔴 Using MO theory, compare O₂, O₂⁺, O₂⁻, and O₂²⁻:
(i) Write the bond orders of each species.
(ii) Arrange them in order of increasing bond length.
(iii) Which of these are paramagnetic?

**Solution:**

The MO configuration for O₂ (16e):
σ1s² σ*1s² σ2s² σ*2s² σ2pz² π2pₓ² π2py² π*2pₓ¹ π*2py¹
→ Nb = 10; Na = 6; b.o. = ½(10−6) = **2**

**(i) Bond orders:**

| Species | Electrons | π* electrons | Nb | Na | b.o.          |
| ------- | --------- | ------------- | -- | -- | ------------- |
| O₂⁺   | 15        | 1 in π*      | 10 | 5  | **2.5** |
| O₂     | 16        | 2 in π*      | 10 | 6  | **2.0** |
| O₂⁻   | 17        | 3 in π*      | 10 | 7  | **1.5** |
| O₂²⁻ | 18        | 4 in π*      | 10 | 8  | **1.0** |

**(ii) Increasing bond length order** (higher b.o. → shorter bond):
**O₂⁺ < O₂ < O₂⁻ < O₂²⁻**

**(iii) Paramagnetic species** (those with unpaired electrons):

* **O₂⁺:** 1 electron in π* → paramagnetic (1 unpaired)
* **O₂:** 2 electrons in π*2pₓ and π*2py (1 each) → **paramagnetic** (2 unpaired)
* **O₂⁻:** 3 electrons in π* (one orbital has 2, other has 1) → **paramagnetic** (1 unpaired)
* **O₂²⁻:** 4 electrons in π* (both π* filled) → **diamagnetic** (all paired)

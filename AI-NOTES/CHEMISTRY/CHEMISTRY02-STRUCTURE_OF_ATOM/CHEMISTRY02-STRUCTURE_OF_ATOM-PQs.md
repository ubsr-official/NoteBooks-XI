**Q54.** 🟡

**Q55.** 🟢

**Assertion (A):** The de Broglie wavelength is experimentally significant for electrons but completely negligible for a cricket ball.

**Reason (R):** de Broglie wavelength is inversely proportional to the mass of the particle; macroscopic objects have such large mass that their associated wavelength is immeasurably small.

*(a) A  (b) B  (c) C  (d) D*

**Q56.** 🔴

**Assertion (A):** It is impossible to simultaneously determine the exact position and exact momentum of an electron.

**Q57.** 🟡

**Assertion (A):** In the hydrogen atom, the 2s and 2p orbitals have exactly the same energy.

**Q58.** 🟡

**Q78.** 🟡 Calculate the energy and wavelength of the photon emitted when an electron in hydrogen atom transitions from n = 4 to n = 2.
(R_H = 2.18 × 10⁻¹⁸ J, h = 6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s, c = 3.0 × 10⁸ m s⁻¹)

**Solution:**

$$
\Delta E = R_H\left(\frac{1}{n_i^2} - \frac{1}{n_f^2}\right) = 2.18\times10^{-18}\left(\frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{16}\right) = 2.18\times10^{-18} \times \frac{3}{16} = 4.09\times10^{-19} \text{ J}
$$

$$
\lambda = \frac{hc}{\Delta E} = \frac{6.626\times10^{-34} \times 3.0\times10^8}{4.09\times10^{-19}} = \boxed{4.86\times10^{-7} \text{ m} = 486 \text{ nm (blue-green, Balmer series)}}
$$

**Q79.** 🟡 A photon of wavelength 400 nm strikes the surface of potassium metal (work function W₀ = 2.25 eV). Calculate: (i) the energy of the photon in eV, (ii) the kinetic energy of the ejected photoelectron, and (iii) the velocity of the photoelectron.
(h = 6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s, c = 3.0 × 10⁸ m s⁻¹, mₑ = 9.1 × 10⁻³¹ kg, 1 eV = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ J)

**Solution:**

**(i)** E = hc/λ = (6.626×10⁻³⁴ × 3.0×10⁸)/(400×10⁻⁹) = 4.97×10⁻¹⁹ J = **3.10 eV**

**(ii)** KE = E − W₀ = 3.10 − 2.25 = **0.85 eV = 1.36 × 10⁻¹⁹ J**

**(iii)** ½mₑv² = 1.36×10⁻¹⁹ J → v² = 2(1.36×10⁻¹⁹)/(9.1×10⁻³¹) = 2.99×10¹¹ → **v = 5.47 × 10⁵ m s⁻¹**

**Q80.** 🟡 Calculate the de Broglie wavelength of:

**(ii)** λ = 6.626×10⁻³⁴/(0.1 × 10) = **6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ m**

**Q82.** 🔴 Calculate the uncertainty in the velocity of an electron confined to a region of size equal to the diameter of a hydrogen atom (Δx = 2 × 52.9 pm = 1.058 × 10⁻¹⁰ m).

$$

$$
= \frac{6.626\times10^{-34}}{1.21\times10^{-39}} = \boxed{5.48\times10^5 \text{ m s}^{-1}}

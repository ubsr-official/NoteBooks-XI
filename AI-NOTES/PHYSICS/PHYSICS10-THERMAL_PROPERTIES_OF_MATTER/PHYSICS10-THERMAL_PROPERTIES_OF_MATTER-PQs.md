# PART B: ASSERTION-REASON QUESTIONS ⭐⭐

> **Instructions:** Select the correct option:
> * **(a)** Both A and R are true and R is the correct explanation of A.
> * **(b)** Both A and R are true but R is NOT the correct explanation of A.
> * **(c)** A is true but R is false.
> * **(d)** A is false but R is true.
> * **(e)** Both A and R are false.

---

**Q41.**
**Assertion (A):** Water in a lake freezes from the top surface downward.
**Reason (R):** Water has maximum density at 4°C, so water at 4°C sinks and water below 4°C becomes less dense and stays near the top.

---

**Q42.**
**Assertion (A):** A burn from steam at 100°C is more severe than a burn from boiling water at 100°C.
**Reason (R):** Steam has a higher temperature than boiling water.

---

**Q43.**
**Assertion (A):** The coefficient of volume expansion of an ideal gas at constant pressure is inversely proportional to its absolute temperature.
**Reason (R):** From PV = µRT at constant P, V ∝ T, so ΔV/V = ΔT/T, giving αv = 1/T.

---

**Q44.**
**Assertion (A):** Two thermometers — one with mercury and one with alcohol — may give slightly different readings for the same temperature (other than fixed points).
**Reason (R):** Mercury and alcohol do not expand linearly in exactly the same way with temperature.

---

**Q45.**
**Assertion (A):** A shiny metallic surface is a poor emitter of heat radiation.
**Reason (R):** A body with high reflectivity absorbs less radiation, and by Kirchhoff's law, it also emits less.

---

**Q46.**
**Assertion (A):** During the melting of ice, the temperature remains constant even though heat is being supplied continuously.
**Reason (R):** The heat supplied during melting goes into increasing the kinetic energy of the molecules.

---

**Q47.**
**Assertion (A):** Cooking in a pressure cooker is faster than in an open vessel.
**Reason (R):** Inside a pressure cooker, pressure is higher, which increases the boiling point of water, allowing food to cook at a higher temperature.

---

**Q48.**
**Assertion (A):** Newton's law of cooling is valid only for small temperature differences.
**Reason (R):** For large temperature differences, the Stefan-Boltzmann T⁴ law gives accurate results, whereas Newton's linear law breaks down.

---

**Q49.**
**Assertion (A):** The triple point of water is used as the standard fixed point in the Kelvin scale rather than the ice point or steam point.
**Reason (R):** The triple point occurs at a unique temperature and pressure, making it a precisely reproducible standard.

---

**Q50.**
**Assertion (A):** Metals are good thermal conductors while non-metals are generally poor conductors.
**Reason (R):** In metals, free electrons carry heat energy rapidly from hot to cold regions, in addition to lattice vibrations.

---

**Q71:**
(a) tC + 273.15
(b) 8.31 J mol⁻¹ K⁻¹

**Q72:**

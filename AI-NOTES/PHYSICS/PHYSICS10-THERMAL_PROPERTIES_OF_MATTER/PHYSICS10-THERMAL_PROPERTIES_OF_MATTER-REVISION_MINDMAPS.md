## 🔢 Key Definitions — Absolute Must-Memorise

| Quantity | Definition | Formula | SI Unit |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Temperature** | Measure of hotness/coldness | — | K (or °C) |
| **Heat** | Energy transferred due to temperature difference | Q = msΔT | J |
| **Specific heat capacity** | Heat per unit mass per unit degree rise | s = Q/(mΔT) | J kg⁻¹ K⁻¹ |
| **Latent heat** | Heat per unit mass during phase change (at constant T) | L = Q/m | J kg⁻¹ |
| **Coefficient of linear expansion** | Fractional change in length per degree | αₗ = (Δl/l)/ΔT | K⁻¹ |
| **Coefficient of volume expansion** | Fractional change in volume per degree | αᵥ = (ΔV/V)/ΔT | K⁻¹ |
| **Thermal conductivity** | Heat conducted per unit area per unit temp gradient | H = KA(ΔT/L) | W m⁻¹ K⁻¹ |
| **Emissivity** | Ratio of actual radiation to blackbody radiation | e = H/AσT⁴ | Dimensionless |

## ⚠️ Critical Distinctions — High-Yield Exam Traps

```
TEMPERATURE/HEAT TRAPS:
  ✓ Heat is NOT stored in a body — it is energy IN TRANSIT due to ΔT
  ✓ Two bodies in thermal equilibrium: same temperature, NOT same heat content
  ✓ Temperature is a SCALAR; it determines direction of heat flow
  ✓ −40°C = −40°F is the ONLY point where Celsius = Fahrenheit
  ✓ Triple point (273.16 K) ≠ melting point of ice at 1 atm (273.15 K ≈ 0 °C)

THERMAL EXPANSION TRAPS:
  ✓ αᵥ = 3αₗ (NOT αᵥ = αₗ or 2αₗ) — THREE dimensions
  ✓ αₐ = 2αₗ (area = TWO dimensions)
  ✓ A HOLE in a material expands JUST LIKE the material itself — the hole gets bigger
  ✓ Water: contracts from 0°C to 4°C (maximum density at 4°C — anomalous!)
  ✓ Ice is LESS dense than water — this is why ice floats (and why bridges don't crack)
  ✓ Thermal stress formula: Y × αₗ × ΔT (needs Young's modulus, NOT bulk modulus)

SPECIFIC HEAT / LATENT HEAT TRAPS:
  ✓ During phase change: T is CONSTANT → ΔT = 0 → Q = mL (NOT msΔT!)
  ✓ Q = msΔT is used ONLY when NO phase change occurs
  ✓ Water has the HIGHEST specific heat of all common substances
  ✓ Cₚ > Cᵥ ALWAYS for gases; Cₚ − Cᵥ = R (for ideal gas)
  ✓ Lv(water) = 22.6×10⁵ J kg⁻¹ — steam burn more severe than boiling water burn

CONDUCTION TRAPS:
  ✓ H (heat current) = same everywhere in STEADY STATE for rods in series
  ✓ Thermal resistance formula: R = L/(KA) — NOT L/K or LK/A
  ✓ Better insulator = LOWER thermal conductivity K
  ✓ Hollow walls with air gaps are good insulators (K_air = 0.024 W m⁻¹ K⁻¹ — very low)

RADIATION TRAPS:
  ✓ Stefan's law: H ∝ T⁴ (NOT T or T² or T³) — very sensitive to temperature!
  ✓ Doubling T → radiation increases 2⁴ = 16 times
  ✓ Wien: T↑ → λm↓ (hotter object → peak at SHORTER wavelength)
  ✓ Good absorbers are also good emitters (Kirchhoff's law of radiation)
  ✓ White/shiny bodies: low absorptivity AND low emissivity
  ✓ Radiation needs NO medium; conduction and convection need matter

NEWTON'S LAW TRAPS:
  ✓ Valid ONLY for small temperature differences (≲40 K over surroundings)
  ✓ Cooling is NOT linear with time — it is EXPONENTIAL
  ✓ Rate of cooling depends on EXCESS temperature, not absolute temperature
  ✓ Slope of ln(T₂−T₁) vs t graph = −K = −k/(ms); negative slope!
  ✓ Newton's law is a LINEAR APPROXIMATION of Stefan's T⁴ law
```

## 🏆 Last-Minute Exam Checklist

```
Before answering any Thermal Properties question:

□ Is it about temperature CONVERSION?
  → Use: tF = (9/5)tC + 32;  T = tC + 273.15
  → Crosspoint: −40°C = −40°F

□ Is it a THERMAL EXPANSION problem?
  → Linear: Δl = αₗ × l × ΔT
  → Area: ΔA = 2αₗ × A × ΔT
  → Volume: ΔV = 3αₗ × V × ΔT (= αᵥ × V × ΔT)
  → Hole in material: EXPANDS just like material (hole gets bigger!)
  → Thermal stress: σ = Y × αₗ × ΔT (if expansion prevented)

□ SPECIFIC HEAT or LATENT HEAT?
  → No phase change: Q = msΔT
  → Phase change: Q = mL (ΔT = 0!)
  → Calorimetry: mH sH ΔTH = mc sc ΔTc
  → swater = 4186 J kg⁻¹ K⁻¹; Lf = 3.33×10⁵; Lv = 22.6×10⁵ J kg⁻¹

□ CONDUCTION problem?
  → H = KA(TC − TD)/L
  → Rods in series: same H through all; find junction temperature T₀
  → T₀ = (K₁A₁T₁/L₁ + K₂A₂T₂/L₂)/(K₁A₁/L₁ + K₂A₂/L₂) [general]
  → If same A and L: T₀ = (K₁T₁ + K₂T₂)/(K₁ + K₂)
  → Rods in parallel: H₁ = K₁A₁ΔT/L; H₂ = K₂A₂ΔT/L; sum them

□ RADIATION problem?
  → Stefan's law: H = AeσT⁴  (note: T must be in KELVIN!)
  → Net: Hnet = Aeσ(T⁴ − Ts⁴)  (both in Kelvin!)
  → Wien's law: λm = 2.9×10⁻³/T  (T in Kelvin; λm in metres)
  → Perfect blackbody: e = 1

□ NEWTON'S LAW OF COOLING?
  → Rate of cooling ∝ (T₂ − T₁) = excess temperature
  → T₂ = T₁ + C′e⁻ᴷᵗ
  → Average method: (ΔT/Δt) = K × (Tmean − T_surroundings)
  → Valid ONLY for small ΔT

□ DIMENSIONAL FORMULA CHECK?
  → s: [L²T⁻²K⁻¹]  (same as L = latent heat: [L²T⁻²])
  → αₗ, αᵥ: [K⁻¹]
  → K (thermal cond.): [MLT⁻³K⁻¹]
  → σ (Stefan): [MT⁻³K⁻⁴]
  → Q, H (energy/heat): [ML²T⁻²]
```

## 📌 Common Formula Errors to Avoid

| Wrong | Correct | Situation |
|---|---|---|
| Q = msΔT during melting | Q = **mL** (ΔT = 0!) | Phase change — no temperature change |
| αᵥ = αₗ or αᵥ = 2αₗ | αᵥ = **3**αₗ | Volume is 3D |
| αₐ = αₗ or αₐ = 3αₗ | αₐ = **2**αₗ | Area is 2D |
| H = K(TC − TD)/L | H = KA(TC − TD)/L | Area A must appear |
| Stefan's H = AeσT⁴ with T in °C | H = AeσT⁴ with **T in Kelvin** | Stefan's law uses absolute temperature |
| Wien: λm × t°C = constant | λm × **T(Kelvin)** = 2.9×10⁻³ | Wien's law uses absolute temperature |
| Cᵥ > Cₚ | **Cₚ > Cᵥ** (always for gases) | At const P, expansion work adds to needed heat |
| Hole in metal shrinks on heating | Hole **expands** (like material) | Material expands outward |
| Rate of cooling linear with time | Rate of cooling is **exponential** | Newton's law → T₂ = T₁ + C′e⁻ᴷᵗ |
| Newton's law valid for any ΔT | Valid only for **small** ΔT | Linear approximation of T⁴ law |

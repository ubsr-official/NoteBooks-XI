**Q46.**
**Assertion (A):** Internal energy U is a state variable.
**Reason (R):** The value of U depends on the path taken to reach the current state, not just on the state itself.

---

**Q47.**
**Assertion (A):** In an isothermal expansion of an ideal gas, no heat is exchanged with the surroundings.
**Reason (R):** For an ideal gas in isothermal process, ΔU = 0, so ΔQ = ΔW (heat absorbed = work done).

---

**Q48.**
**Assertion (A):** A steam engine can never have 100% efficiency.
**Reason (R):** The Second Law of Thermodynamics forbids a heat engine from converting all absorbed heat into work.

---

**Q49.**
**Assertion (A):** An adiabatic curve on a P-V diagram is steeper than an isothermal curve at any given point.
**Reason (R):** In an adiabatic process, PVᵞ = constant, and since γ > 1, the pressure drops faster per unit volume increase than in an isothermal process.

---

**Q50.**
**Assertion (A):** The Zeroth Law of Thermodynamics is less fundamental than the First or Second Laws.
**Reason (R):** The Zeroth Law was formulated after the First and Second Laws, which is why it is called 'Zeroth'.

---

**Q51.**
**Assertion (A):** All reversible processes are quasi-static.
**Reason (R):** A quasi-static process involves non-equilibrium states that can be reversed.

---

**Q52.**
**Assertion (A):** The efficiency of a Carnot engine depends on the nature of the working substance used.
**Reason (R):** Carnot's theorem states that the efficiency is independent of the working substance and depends only on the temperatures of the source and sink.

---

**Q53.**
**Assertion (A):** During free expansion of an ideal gas into vacuum, temperature does not change.
**Reason (R):** In free expansion: no work is done (W = 0), no heat exchange occurs (Q = 0), so ΔU = 0, and since U depends only on T for an ideal gas, T is unchanged.

---

**Q54.**
**Assertion (A):** Cₚ > Cᵥ for all gases.
**Reason (R):** At constant pressure, additional heat must supply both the internal energy increase and the work done by the gas in expansion.

---

**Q55.**
**Assertion (A):** The Clausius and Kelvin-Planck statements of the Second Law are independent of each other.
**Reason (R):** The two statements are completely equivalent — each can be derived from the other.

**Q72.** State True or False and correct the false ones:

(a) Heat is a state variable of a thermodynamic system.
(b) For an ideal gas, internal energy U depends only on temperature T.
(c) In an isochoric process, work done is zero and ΔQ = ΔU.
(d) The efficiency of a Carnot engine can exceed 1 if the source temperature is very high.
(e) An adiabatic process and an isothermal process have the same slope on a P-V diagram.
(f) All quasi-static processes are reversible.
(g) In a cyclic process, the total heat absorbed by the system equals the total work done.
(h) The Clausius and Kelvin-Planck statements of the Second Law are independent of each other.
(i) Cₚ > Cᵥ for all gases, with Cₚ − Cᵥ = R for an ideal gas.
(j) A Carnot engine is the only reversible engine possible between two temperatures.

---

### ✅ D ANSWERS

**Q71:**
(a) ΔU + ΔW
(b) ΔU = 0; ΔQ = ΔW (= μRT ln(V₂/V₁))
(c) ΔQ = 0; constant
(d) R (universal gas constant = 8.31 J mol⁻¹ K⁻¹)
(e) 1 − T₂/T₁
(f) 3R ≈ 24.9 J mol⁻¹ K⁻¹
(g) ΔU = 0
(h) Isothermal expansion (T₁) → Adiabatic expansion → Isothermal compression (T₂) → Adiabatic compression
(i) T₁/T₂
(j) quasi-static AND free of dissipative effects (non-dissipative)

**Q72:**
(a) **False** — Heat is a *path function* (NOT a state variable). State variables: U, P, V, T.
(b) **True** — Key property of ideal gas: U = U(T) only.
(c) **True** — ΔW = PΔV = 0 at constant V; therefore ΔQ = ΔU.
(d) **False** — η = 1 − T₂/T₁ ≤ 1 always (since T₂ ≥ 0 and T₁ > 0). η approaches 1 only when T₂ → 0 K, never exceeds 1.
(e) **False** — Adiabat is *steeper* than isotherm at any given point (slope ratio = γ > 1).
(f) **False** — Quasi-static is *necessary* but not *sufficient* for reversibility. A quasi-static process with friction is still irreversible.
(g) **True** — Cyclic process: ΔU = 0 → ΔQ = ΔW.
(h) **False** — The two statements are *equivalent* — each can be derived from the other.
(i) **True** — Mayer's relation: Cₚ − Cᵥ = R for ideal gas.
(j) **True** — A Carnot engine is the unique reversible engine operating between two fixed temperatures.

### ✅ E ANSWERS

**Q73.**
(a) η = 1 − T₂/T₁ = 1 − 300/600 = **0.5 = 50%**
(b) W = ηQ₁ = 0.5 × 4000 = **2000 J**
(c) Q₂ = Q₁ − W = 4000 − 2000 = **2000 J**

**Q74.**
(a) Adiabatic: TV^(γ−1) = const → T₁V₁^(γ−1) = T₂V₂^(γ−1)
600 × V₁^(2/3) = T₂ × (2V₁)^(2/3) → T₂ = 600/2^(2/3) = 600/1.587 ≈ **378 K**

(b) W = μR(T₁−T₂)/(γ−1) = 1 × 8.3 × (600−378)/(5/3−1)
= 8.3 × 222 / (2/3) = 8.3 × 222 × 1.5 = **2764 J ≈ 2.76 kJ**

**Q75.**
(a) Isobaric: V/T = constant → V₁/T₁ = 2V₁/T₂ → T₂ = **2T₁ = 2 × 300 = 600 K**

(b) ΔQ = μCₚΔT = 2 × (5/2 × 8.3) × (600−300) = 2 × 20.75 × 300 = **12,450 J ≈ 12.45 kJ**

(c) ΔW = μRΔT = 2 × 8.3 × 300 = **4980 J ≈ 4.98 kJ**

(d) ΔU = μCᵥΔT = 2 × (3/2 × 8.3) × 300 = 2 × 12.45 × 300 = **7470 J ≈ 7.47 kJ**
✓ Check: ΔQ = ΔU + ΔW → 12,450 = 7470 + 4980 ✓

**Q76.**
ΔQ/Δt = 100 W (heat supplied); ΔW/Δt = 75 J/s (work done by system)
Rate of ΔU = ΔQ/Δt − ΔW/Δt = 100 − 75 = **25 W (25 J/s)**

**Q77.**
Adiabatic path A→B: ΔQ = 0; W_on = 22.3 J → ΔU_adiab = +22.3 J
(Work done ON system → internal energy increases by 22.3 J)
∴ ΔU (A→B) = **+22.3 J** (same regardless of path, since U is state variable)

Second path (net heat absorbed = 9.35 cal = 9.35 × 4.19 = 39.18 J):
ΔQ − ΔW = ΔU → 39.18 − ΔW = 22.3
∴ ΔW = 39.18 − 22.3 = **16.88 J ≈ 16.9 J**

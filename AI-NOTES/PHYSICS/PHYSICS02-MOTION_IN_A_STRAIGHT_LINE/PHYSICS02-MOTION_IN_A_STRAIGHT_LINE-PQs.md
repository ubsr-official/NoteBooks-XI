# PART B: ASSERTION–REASON QUESTIONS ⭐⭐

---

> **Instructions:**
>
> * (a) Both A and R are true, and R is the correct explanation of A.
> * (b) Both A and R are true, but R is NOT the correct explanation of A.
> * (c) A is true, but R is false.
> * (d) A is false, but R is true.

---

**Q46.** 🟢
**Assertion (A):** The displacement of an object can be zero even when distance travelled is not zero.
**Reason (R):** Displacement is the net change in position, while distance is the total path length.

---

**Q47.** 🟢
**Assertion (A):** The average speed of a body is always greater than or equal to the magnitude of average velocity.
**Reason (R):** Speed is a scalar while velocity is a vector.

---

**Q48.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** A body with non-zero acceleration can have zero velocity.
**Reason (R):** Velocity and acceleration are independent quantities.

---

**Q49.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** The speedometer of a car reads the instantaneous speed of the car.
**Reason (R):** Instantaneous speed is equal to the magnitude of instantaneous velocity.

---

**Q50.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** Negative acceleration always means the object is slowing down.
**Reason (R):** The sign of acceleration depends on the choice of positive direction of the axis.

---

**Q51.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** An object in free fall has constant acceleration.
**Reason (R):** Near the Earth's surface, g varies significantly with height.

---

**Q52.** 🔴
**Assertion (A):** The area under the v–t graph between t₁ and t₂ gives the distance covered (total path length) in that interval.
**Reason (R):** Distance is the integral of the magnitude of velocity over time.

---

**Q53.** 🔴
**Assertion (A):** For a body moving with constant velocity, the x–t graph is a straight line not necessarily passing through the origin.
**Reason (R):** A straight x–t graph indicates a constant slope, which means constant velocity.

---

**Q54.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** Two bodies A and B are moving in the same direction with speeds 10 m s⁻¹ and 15 m s⁻¹ respectively. The magnitude of relative velocity of A with respect to B is 5 m s⁻¹.
**Reason (R):** Relative velocity = v_A − v_B when both move in the same direction.

---

**Q55.** 🔴
**Assertion (A):** In the kinematic equation v² = v₀² + 2ax, the equation is valid even if acceleration is varying, as long as 'a' represents the average acceleration.
**Reason (R):** Kinematic equations are derived under the assumption of constant acceleration only.

**Q78.** (Numerical) A car moving at 90 km h⁻¹ is brought to rest in 200 m. Find (i) the retardation and (ii) the time taken to stop.

**Answer:**

```
u = 90 km/h = 25 m/s; v = 0; s = 200 m
(i) v² = u² + 2as → 0 = 625 + 2a(200) → a = −625/400 = −1.5625 m s⁻²
(ii) v = u + at → 0 = 25 − 1.5625t → t = 16 s
```

**Q79.** (Numerical) Two cars start simultaneously from the same point on a straight road. Car 1 moves with constant velocity 20 m s⁻¹. Car 2 starts from rest with acceleration 2 m s⁻². **Answer:**

```
(i) x₁ = x₂: 20t = ½(2)t² → t² − 20t = 0 → t(t−20) = 0
    t = 20 s (non-zero solution)

(ii) Distance = 20 × 20 = 400 m

(iii) v₂ = 0 + 2(20) = 40 m s⁻¹ (twice the speed of Car 1!)
```

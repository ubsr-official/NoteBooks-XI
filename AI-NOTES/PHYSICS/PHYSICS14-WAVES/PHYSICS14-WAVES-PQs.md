**Q74.** State True or False and correct the false ones:

(a) The speed of sound in a gas increases when pressure is doubled at constant temperature.
(b) Transverse waves can travel through water.
(c) At an open end of a pipe, a displacement antinode is formed.
(d) The fundamental frequency of a pipe open at both ends is twice that of a pipe closed at one end of the same length.
(e) Beats can be heard when two waves have a frequency difference of 500 Hz.
(f) In a standing wave, energy is continuously transported from node to antinode.
(g) Newton assumed the propagation of sound in a gas to be an adiabatic process.
(h) The wavelength of a wave changes when it passes from one medium to another.
(i) All harmonics are present in a closed organ pipe.
(j) A displacement node is a pressure antinode in a sound wave.

---

### ✅ D ANSWERS

**Q73:**
(a) y = a sin(kx − ωt + φ)
(b) k = 2π/λ
(c) v = ω/k
(d) v = √(T/µ)
(e) v = √(γP/ρ)
(f) y = 2a sin kx cos ωt
(g) νₙ = nv/2L, n = 1, 2, 3, ...
(h) ν₀ = v/4L
(i) ν_beat = |ν₁ − ν₂|
(j) π (180°) phase reversal

**Q74:**
(a) **False** — At constant temperature, P/ρ = RT/M = constant. Doubling pressure also doubles density, so v = √(γP/ρ) is **unchanged**.
(b) **False** — Transverse mechanical waves require **shear modulus**. Water (a liquid) does not have shear modulus → transverse waves **cannot** travel through bulk water. (Surface waves are different.)
(c) **True** — The open end is free to vibrate maximally → **displacement antinode**.
(d) **True** — Open pipe fundamental = v/2L; closed pipe fundamental = v/4L. Open/closed ratio = 2:1 → open is twice that of closed.
(e) **False** — A frequency difference of 500 Hz is far too large. The human ear can detect beats only when the frequency difference is approximately **15–20 Hz or less**. At 500 Hz difference, only a harsh dissonance is heard.
(f) **False** — Standing waves do **not** transport energy. Energy is stored locally and oscillates between KE and PE at each point, but there is no net flow of energy.
(g) **False** — Newton assumed propagation to be **isothermal**. It was **Laplace** who identified it as adiabatic and made the correction.
(h) **True** — When a wave passes to a different medium, speed changes but frequency stays constant → wavelength = v/ν changes.
(i) **False** — A closed organ pipe supports **only odd harmonics** (1st, 3rd, 5th, ...). Even harmonics are absent.
(j) **True** — Where displacement is zero (particles stationary), pressure accumulates maximally → **displacement node = pressure antinode**.

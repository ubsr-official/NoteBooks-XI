**Q41.** 🟢
**Assertion (A):** The escape speed from the Moon is much less than from Earth.
**Reason (R):** The Moon has smaller mass and smaller radius compared to Earth.

---

**Q42.** 🟢
**Assertion (A):** A planet moves faster when it is closer to the Sun.
**Reason (R):** The gravitational force from the Sun increases as the planet moves closer.

---

**Q43.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** An astronaut inside an orbiting space station experiences weightlessness.
**Reason (R):** The gravitational force of Earth is zero at the orbital altitude of the space station.

---

**Q44.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** Gravitational potential energy of a satellite is negative.
**Reason (R):** Gravitational force is attractive and the reference level (V = 0) is set at infinity.

---

**Q45.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** The total mechanical energy of an orbiting satellite is negative.
**Reason (R):** The kinetic energy of the satellite is greater than the magnitude of its potential energy.

---

**Q46.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** Kepler's second law holds only for gravitational force.
**Reason (R):** The second law is equivalent to conservation of angular momentum.

---

**Q47.** 🔴
**Assertion (A):** If Earth were to shrink to half its radius (mass unchanged), the escape speed from its surface would increase.
**Reason (R):** Escape speed is inversely proportional to the square root of the radius.

---

**Q48.** 🔴
**Assertion (A):** When a satellite is transferred to a higher circular orbit, its speed decreases but its total energy increases (becomes less negative).
**Reason (R):** For a satellite in circular orbit, KE = |E| = GMEm/2(RE+h); higher h means smaller KE and less negative total energy E.

---

**Q49.** 🔴
**Assertion (A):** Gravitational shielding is impossible.
**Reason (R):** Gravitational force, unlike electromagnetic force, cannot be cancelled by a conductor or shell.

---

**Q50.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** The gravitational potential energy near Earth's surface can be written as mgh.
**Reason (R):** For h ≪ RE, the gravitational potential energy difference is approximately mgh.

| 42 | (a) | Both true. Kepler's 2nd law (ΔA/Δt = const) means closer → sweeps same area faster → higher v. Higher F provides the needed centripetal force for faster orbital motion. |

**Q71.** 🟢 Calculate the gravitational force between Earth (ME = 6×10²⁴ kg) and the Moon (Mm = 7.4×10²² kg) if the distance between their centres is 3.84×10⁸ m. (G = 6.67×10⁻¹¹ N m² kg⁻²)

**Answer:**

$$F = \frac{GM_E M_m}{r^2} = \frac{6.67\times10^{-11} \times 6\times10^{24} \times 7.4\times10^{22}}{(3.84\times10^8)^2}$$

$$= \frac{6.67 \times 6 \times 7.4 \times 10^{35}}{14.75 \times 10^{16}} = \frac{296.1 \times 10^{35}}{14.75 \times 10^{16}} \approx \mathbf{2.01 \times 10^{20} \text{ N}}$$

**Q72.** 🟢 The radius of Earth is 6400 km and g = 9.8 m s⁻². Find the mass of Earth.

**Answer:**

$$M_E = \frac{gR_E^2}{G} = \frac{9.8 \times (6.4\times10^6)^2}{6.67\times10^{-11}} = \frac{9.8 \times 4.096\times10^{13}}{6.67\times10^{-11}} = \frac{4.014\times10^{14}}{6.67\times10^{-11}} \approx \mathbf{6.02\times10^{24} \text{ kg}}$$

**Q73.** 🟢 A body weighs 63 N on Earth's surface. Find the gravitational force on it at a height equal to half the radius of Earth. (RE = 6400 km)

**Answer:**

$$g(h) = \frac{g_0}{(1+h/R_E)^2} = \frac{g_0}{(1+0.5)^2} = \frac{g_0}{2.25}$$

Weight at height = 63/2.25 = **28 N**

**Q74.** 🟢 Assuming Earth is a sphere of uniform density, find the weight of a body halfway down to the centre of Earth if it weighs 250 N on the surface.

**Answer:**

$$g(d) = g_0\left(1 - \frac{d}{R_E}\right) = g_0\left(1 - \frac{1}{2}\right) = \frac{g_0}{2}$$

Weight at depth = 250/2 = **125 N**

**Q75.** 🟡 A planet orbits the Sun with time period 1.88 Earth years. What is its orbital radius in AU? (Earth-Sun distance = 1 AU)

**Answer:**

$$T^2 \propto a^3 \Rightarrow \frac{T_P^2}{T_E^2} = \frac{a_P^3}{a_E^3}$$

$$a_P^3 = (1.88)^2 \times 1^3 = 3.534$$

$$a_P = (3.534)^{1/3} = \mathbf{1.52 \text{ AU}}$$

**Q76.** 🟡 Find the escape speed from the surface of Mars. Given: mass of Mars = 6.4×10²³ kg, radius of Mars = 3.4×10⁶ m, G = 6.67×10⁻¹¹ N m² kg⁻².

**Q77.** 🟡 A satellite orbits Earth at a height of 400 km. How much energy must be supplied to rocket it out of Earth's gravitational field? (m = 200 kg, ME = 6×10²⁴ kg, RE = 6.4×10⁶ m, G = 6.67×10⁻¹¹ N m² kg⁻²)

**Q78.** 🔴 Two stars, each of mass M = 2×10³⁰ kg, approach each other for a head-on collision. At separation d = 10⁹ km their speeds are negligible. Find the speed of each star just before collision. (radius of each star = 10⁴ km, G = 6.67×10⁻¹¹ N m² kg⁻²)

**Answer:**

Energy conservation (initial KE ≈ 0; initial PE = −GM²/d; final PE = −GM²/2R_star):

$$\frac{1}{2}(2M)v^2 = GM^2\left(\frac{1}{2R}-\frac{1}{d}\right)$$

Since d = 10⁹ km ≫ 2R_star = 2×10⁴ km, the term 1/d is negligible:

$$v \approx \sqrt{\frac{GM}{2R_{star}}} = \sqrt{\frac{6.67\times10^{-11}\times2\times10^{30}}{2\times10^7}} = \sqrt{6.67\times10^{12}} \approx \mathbf{2.58\times10^6 \text{ m s}^{-1} \approx 2580 \text{ km s}^{-1}}$$

**Q79.** 🔴 A rocket is fired vertically with a speed of 5 km s⁻¹ from Earth's surface. How far from Earth does it go before returning? (ME = 6×10²⁴ kg, RE = 6.4×10⁶ m, G = 6.67×10⁻¹¹ N m² kg⁻²)

**Answer:**

Energy conservation: ½mv² − GMEm/RE = −GMEm/r_max (at max height, v = 0):

$$r_{max} = \frac{GM_E}{\frac{GM_E}{R_E} - \frac{v^2}{2}} = \frac{R_E}{1 - \frac{v^2 R_E}{2GM_E}}$$

v² = (5000)² = 25×10⁶ m² s⁻²; v²RE/2GME = 25×10⁶ × 6.4×10⁶/(2 × 6.67×10⁻¹¹ × 6×10²⁴) = 1.6×10¹⁴/8×10¹⁴ = 0.2

$$r_{max} = \frac{6.4\times10^6}{1-0.2} = \frac{6.4\times10^6}{0.8} = 8\times10^6 \text{ m}$$

Height above surface = rmax − RE = 8×10⁶ − 6.4×10⁶ = **1.6×10⁶ m = 1600 km**

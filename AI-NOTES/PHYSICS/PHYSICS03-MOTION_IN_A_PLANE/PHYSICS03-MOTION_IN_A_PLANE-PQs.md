**Q46.** 🟢
**Assertion (A):** The displacement vector of a particle moving in a plane does not depend on the actual path taken.
**Reason (R):** Displacement is the shortest distance between initial and final positions.

---

**Q47.** 🟢
**Assertion (A):** The horizontal component of velocity of a projectile remains constant throughout its flight.
**Reason (R):** The horizontal acceleration of a projectile is zero (neglecting air resistance).

---

**Q48.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** The speed of a particle in uniform circular motion is constant, but its velocity is not constant.
**Reason (R):** In uniform circular motion, the direction of velocity changes continuously although its magnitude remains unchanged.

---

**Q49.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** The path of a projectile is parabolic only when the angle of projection is 45°.
**Reason (R):** The equation of trajectory y = (tanθ)x − gx²/[2(v₀cosθ)²] represents a parabola for any angle θ.

---

**Q50.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** For two complementary angles of projection (θ and 90°−θ), the ranges are equal.
**Reason (R):** sin 2θ = sin(180°−2θ) = sin(2(90°−θ)).

---

**Q51.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** Centripetal acceleration in uniform circular motion is a constant acceleration.
**Reason (R):** The magnitude of centripetal acceleration v²/R is constant in uniform circular motion.

---

**Q52.** 🔴
**Assertion (A):** The kinematic equations v = v₀ + at, s = v₀t + ½at² are NOT applicable to uniform circular motion.
**Reason (R):** In uniform circular motion, the acceleration is not constant (its direction changes continuously).

---

**Q53.** 🔴
**Assertion (A):** Two projectiles projected at angles (45°+α) and (45°−α) with the same initial speed have the same range.
**Reason (R):** The range depends on sin 2θ, and sin(90°+2α) = sin(90°−2α) = cos 2α.

---

**Q54.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** The angle between velocity and centripetal acceleration in uniform circular motion is always 90°.
**Reason (R):** In uniform circular motion, velocity is always tangent to the circle, and centripetal acceleration is always radially inward.

---

**Q55.** 🔴
**Assertion (A):** A vector and its negative have the same magnitude but are not equal.
**Reason (R):** Two vectors are equal only if they have the same magnitude and the same direction. The negative vector has opposite direction.

**Q75.** A football is kicked at 25 m s⁻¹ at 37° above horizontal. Find (a) the maximum height, (b) the time of flight, and (c) the horizontal range. (sin 37° = 0.6, cos 37° = 0.8, g = 10 m s⁻²)

**Answer:**

```
(a) hₘ = vₒᵧ²/(2g) = 225/(20) = 11.25 m

(b) Tƒ = 2vₒᵧ/g = 2×15/10 = 3 s

(c) R = vₒₓ × Tƒ = 20×3 = 60 m
    Check: R = v₀²sin2θ/g = 625×sin74°/10 = 625×0.96/10 = 60 m ✓
```

**Q76.** A particle moves in x-y plane with position vector **r**(t) = (2t²)î + (3t − t²)ĵ metres. Find (a) the velocity at t = 2 s, (b) the acceleration at any time t.

**Answer:**
```
(a) v = dr/dt = (4t)î + (3−2t)ĵ
    At t = 2: v = 8î + (3−4)ĵ = 8î − ĵ m s⁻¹
    |v| = √(64+1) = √65 ≈ 8.06 m s⁻¹

(b) a = dv/dt = 4î + (−2)ĵ = 4î − 2ĵ m s⁻²  (constant)
    |a| = √(16+4) = √20 = 2√5 m s⁻²
```

**Q77.** A satellite moves in a circular orbit of radius 6400 km + 600 km = 7000 km from Earth's centre with speed 7.6 km s⁻¹. Find (a) the angular speed and (b) the centripetal acceleration.

**Answer:**
```
R = 7×10⁶ m;  v = 7600 m s⁻¹

(a) ω = v/R = 7600/(7×10⁶) = 1.086×10⁻³ rad s⁻¹
    T = 2π/ω ≈ 5786 s ≈ 96.4 min

(b) aₒ = v²/R = (7600)²/(7×10⁶) = 57760000/7000000 ≈ 8.25 m s⁻²
    (≈ 0.84g — makes sense as gravity weakens at altitude)
```

**Q78.** Two vectors **A** and **B** have magnitudes 5 and 3 respectively. Their resultant has magnitude 7. Find the angle between **A** and **B**.

**Answer:**
```
R² = A² + B² + 2AB cosθ
49 = 25 + 9 + 30 cosθ
49 = 34 + 30 cosθ
cosθ = 15/30 = 1/2
θ = 60°
```

**Q79.** A cricketer can throw a ball to a maximum horizontal distance of 100 m. How high above the ground can the cricketer throw the same ball? (g = 10 m s⁻²)

**Answer:**
```
Max range: Rₘₐₓ = v₀²/g = 100 → v₀² = 1000 m² s⁻²

For max height (throw vertically upward, θ = 90°):
hₘₐₓ = v₀²/(2g) = 1000/20 = 50 m

So the cricketer can throw the ball 50 m high — HALF the maximum range!
This is a standard result: hₘₐₓ = Rₘₐₓ/2
```

**Q80.** A motorboat is racing north at 25 km h⁻¹. The water current is 10 km h⁻¹ in the direction 60° east of south. Find the resultant velocity of the boat. (NCERT Example 3.3)

**Answer:**
```
Boat velocity: v_b = 25 km h⁻¹ (north) = 25ĵ
Current velocity: v_c = 10 km h⁻¹ at 60° east of south
  v_cₓ = 10 sin60° = 5√3 km h⁻¹ (east)
  v_cᵧ = −10 cos60° = −5 km h⁻¹ (south)

Resultant:
  Rₓ = 5√3 ≈ 8.66 km h⁻¹
  Rᵧ = 25 − 5 = 20 km h⁻¹

|R| = √((5√3)²+20²) = √(75+400) = √475 ≈ 21.8 ≈ 22 km h⁻¹

Direction (using law of sines): φ ≈ 23.4° east of north
```

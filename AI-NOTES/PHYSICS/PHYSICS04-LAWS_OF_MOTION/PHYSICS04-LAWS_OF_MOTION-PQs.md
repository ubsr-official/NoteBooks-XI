# PART B: ASSERTION–REASON QUESTIONS

> **Instructions:**
> * **(a)** Both A and R are true; R is the correct explanation of A.
> * **(b)** Both A and R are true; R is NOT the correct explanation of A.
> * **(c)** A is true; R is false.
> * **(d)** A is false; R may be true or false.

---

**Q41.** 🟢
**Assertion (A):** A body at rest and a body moving at constant velocity are both in the same dynamical state.
**Reason (R):** Both have zero net external force acting on them.

---

**Q42.** 🟢
**Assertion (A):** Impulse and momentum have the same SI unit.
**Reason (R):** Impulse = change in momentum = FΔt, and both have dimensions [MLT⁻¹].

---

**Q43.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** A horse and cart system accelerates forward even though the cart exerts a force on the horse equal and opposite to what the horse exerts on the cart.
**Reason (R):** Newton's third law forces act on different bodies; the net forward force on the horse comes from ground friction on its hooves, which is external to the horse-cart system... but actually the system accelerates because ground friction > zero. The net external force on the system is ground friction on the horse's feet.

---

**Q44.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** Kinetic friction between two bodies is always less than the maximum static friction between them.
**Reason (R):** When surfaces slide over each other, the effective interlocking of surface irregularities decreases compared to the impending-motion (static) condition.

---

**Q45.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** When a car moves along a circular banked road at optimum speed, no friction acts between the tyres and road.
**Reason (R):** At optimum speed, the horizontal component of the normal reaction exactly provides the necessary centripetal force, so friction is not needed.

---

**Q46.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** The weight of a person in a freely falling lift is zero.
**Reason (R):** In free fall, both person and lift have the same downward acceleration g, making the normal reaction (apparent weight) zero.

---

**Q47.** 🔴
**Assertion (A):** Newton's first law is not a special case of the second law.
**Reason (R):** The first law defines inertial frames of reference; the second law is applied within such frames. They serve different conceptual roles.

---

**Q48.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** The total momentum of an isolated system is conserved even if kinetic energy is lost in a collision.
**Reason (R):** Conservation of momentum follows from Newton's second and third laws alone, independent of energy considerations.

---

**Q49.** 🔴
**Assertion (A):** If a body just slides on an inclined plane at angle θ, then μs = tanθ regardless of the body's mass.
**Reason (R):** At limiting equilibrium, mg sinθ = μs × mg cosθ; mass cancels from both sides.

---

**Q50.** 🔴
**Assertion (A):** Centripetal force does no work on a body moving in a uniform circular path.
**Reason (R):** The centripetal force is always perpendicular to the velocity of the body; work = F·d·cosθ = 0 when θ = 90°.

**Q71.** 🟢 A force of 100 N acts on a 20 kg body initially at rest for 5 s. Find (a) the acceleration, (b) final velocity, and (c) distance covered.

**Answer:**
(a) a = F/m = 100/20 = **5 m s⁻²**
(b) v = u + at = 0 + 5×5 = **25 m s⁻¹**
(c) s = ½at² = ½×5×25 = **62.5 m**

**Q72.** 🟢 A shell of mass 100 g is fired from a 10 kg gun with muzzle velocity 300 m s⁻¹. Find the recoil velocity of the gun.

**Answer:**
Momentum conservation: 0 = 0.1×300 + 10×v_gun
v_gun = −3 m s⁻¹ → **Recoil speed = 3 m s⁻¹** (opposite to bullet)

**Q73.** 🟡 A 5 kg body on a rough horizontal floor has μs = 0.4, μk = 0.3. A horizontal force of 30 N is applied. Does it move? If yes, find acceleration. (g = 10 m s⁻²)

**Answer:**
(fs)max = 0.4 × 5 × 10 = **20 N** < 30 N → **body moves**
fk = 0.3 × 50 = 15 N
a = (30−15)/5 = **3 m s⁻²**

**Q74.** 🟡 A cyclist at 36 km/h takes a circular turn of radius 20 m. If μs = 0.5, will the cyclist slip? (g = 10 m s⁻²)

**Answer:**
v = 10 m s⁻¹; vmax = √(0.5×20×10) = √100 = **10 m s⁻¹**
v = vmax → cyclist is exactly at the limit. **On the verge of slipping (just doesn't slip).**

**Q75.** 🔴 A block of mass 5 kg rests on a horizontal floor (μs = 0.5, μk = 0.4). A rope pulls it at 37° above horizontal with force F. Find (a) minimum F to just start movement, (b) acceleration once it moves. (g = 10 m s⁻²; sin37° = 0.6, cos37° = 0.8)

**Answer:**
N = mg − F sin37° = 50 − 0.6F
(fs)max = μs × N = 0.5(50 − 0.6F)

At impending motion: F cos37° = (fs)max
0.8F = 25 − 0.3F → 1.1F = 25 → **(a) F = 22.73 N**

Once moving:
N = 50 − 0.6×22.73 = 50 − 13.64 = 36.36 N
fk = 0.4 × 36.36 = 14.54 N
Net force = 22.73×0.8 − 14.54 = 18.18 − 14.54 = 3.64 N
**(b) a = 3.64/5 = 0.73 m s⁻²**

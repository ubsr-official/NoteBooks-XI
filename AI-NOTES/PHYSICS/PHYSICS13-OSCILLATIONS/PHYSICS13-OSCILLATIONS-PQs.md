**Q41.**
**Assertion (A):** The time period of a simple pendulum is independent of the mass of the bob.

**Reason (R):** In the equation T = 2π√(L/g), the mass m does not appear because it cancels in Newton's second law for rotational motion (mL²α = −mgL sinθ → α = −g sinθ/L).

---

**Q42.**
**Assertion (A):** At the mean position in SHM, the kinetic energy is maximum and potential energy is zero.

**Reason (R):** At the mean position, the restoring force is zero and the particle has its greatest speed.

---

**Q43.**
**Assertion (A):** The function e^(−ωt) represents a periodic motion.

**Reason (R):** e^(−ωt) decreases monotonically and never repeats its value.

---

**Q44.**
**Assertion (A):** The time period of a spring-mass system remains the same whether it is placed on Earth or on the Moon.

**Reason (R):** The time period T = 2π√(m/k) depends only on mass and spring constant, not on gravitational acceleration g.

---

**Q45.**
**Assertion (A):** In SHM, the total mechanical energy remains constant with time.

**Reason (R):** The restoring force in SHM (F = −kx) is a conservative force, and conservative forces conserve mechanical energy.

---

**Q46.**
**Assertion (A):** A simple pendulum brought from Earth to the Moon will have a smaller time period on the Moon.

**Reason (R):** The acceleration due to gravity on the Moon is less than on Earth.

---

**Q47.**
**Assertion (A):** In SHM, both kinetic and potential energies are always positive (non-negative).

**Reason (R):** KE = ½mv² ≥ 0 always; PE = ½kx² ≥ 0 by choice of zero reference at mean position. Neither can be negative.

---

**Q48.**
**Assertion (A):** The motion sin ωt − cos ωt is simple harmonic.

**Reason (R):** sin ωt − cos ωt = √2 sin(ωt − π/4), which is a sinusoidal function of time with angular frequency ω.

---

**Q49.**
**Assertion (A):** The period of KE in SHM is T/2 (half the period of displacement).

**Reason (R):** KE = ½kA² sin²(ωt + φ) = ½kA²[1 − cos 2(ωt + φ)]/2, which has angular frequency 2ω and thus period π/ω = T/2.

---

**Q50.**
**Assertion (A):** A particle executing SHM with displacement x = A cos(ωt) has acceleration always directed towards the origin.

**Reason (R):** The acceleration a = −ω²x is opposite in sign to x, so it always points towards x = 0 (the mean position).

### ✅ D ANSWERS

**Q71:**
(a) ω = 2π/T (or ω = 2πν)
(b) v = ±ω√(A²−x²)
(c) v_max = ωA
(d) a_max = ω²A
(e) T = 2π√(m/k)

**Q72:**
(a) **True** — Every oscillatory motion repeats at regular intervals (to-and-fro implies period).
(b) **False** — T = 2π√(L/g) has no m; pendulum period is **independent** of bob mass.
(c) **False** — At the mean position (x = 0), acceleration = −ω²×0 = **zero**. Acceleration is maximum at extreme positions (x = ±A).
(d) **True** — E = ½kA²; E ∝ A² (doubling A quadruples E).
(e) **True** — v = −ωA sin(ωt+φ), x = A cos(ωt+φ): phase difference = π/2.
(f) **False** — e^(−ωt) is **non-periodic** (decreases monotonically; represents exponential decay/damping).

(i) **False** — KE and PE each have period **T/2** (half the displacement period). They complete 2 cycles per oscillation of the body.

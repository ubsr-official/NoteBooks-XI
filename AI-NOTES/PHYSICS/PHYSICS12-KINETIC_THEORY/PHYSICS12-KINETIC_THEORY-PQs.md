**Q43.**
**Assertion (A):** The molar specific heat of a diatomic gas (like Cl₂) is sometimes greater than the predicted value of 5R/2.

* (a) (b) (c) (d) (e)

**Q44.**
**Assertion (A):** The mean free path of a gas molecule decreases when pressure is increased at constant temperature.

**Reason (R):** Increasing pressure at constant temperature increases the number density n; since l ∝ 1/n, the mean free path decreases.

* (a) (b) (c) (d) (e)

**Q45.**
**Assertion (A):** The ideal gas equation PV = μRT applies to all real gases at all conditions.

**Reason (R):** At low pressures and high temperatures, real gases approach ideal behaviour because intermolecular forces become negligible.

* (a) (b) (c) (d) (e)

**Q46.**
**Assertion (A):** Carbon (diamond) is an exception to the Dulong-Petit law at room temperature.

**Reason (R):** The vibrational modes of carbon atoms in diamond have such high frequencies that they are quantum-mechanically frozen at room temperature.

* (a) (b) (c) (d) (e)

**Q47.**
**Assertion (A):** At the same temperature, the average kinetic energy per molecule of O₂ is more than that of monoatomic Ar, because O₂ has more degrees of freedom.

**Q48.**
**Assertion (A):** Gas molecules in a room do not all settle to the floor despite the pull of gravity.

**Q49.**
**Assertion (A):** A gas leaking from a cylinder in a kitchen takes considerable time to diffuse to the other corners of the room, even though the molecules are very fast (~500 m/s).

**Reason (R):** Molecules have a finite size and undergo frequent collisions, resulting in a mean free path much smaller than the room dimensions, causing a diffusion-like (random walk) spread rather than straight-line motion.

* (a) (b) (c) (d) (e)

**Q50.**
**Assertion (A):** The rotation of a diatomic molecule along the axis joining the two atoms does not contribute to its energy or degrees of freedom at ordinary temperatures.

**Reason (R):** The moment of inertia about this axis is extremely small; by quantum mechanics, the corresponding rotational energy levels have very large spacing and are not excited at ordinary temperatures.

* (a) (b) (c) (d) (e)

**Q72.** State True or False and correct the false ones:

(a) Temperature is a measure of the total energy (kinetic + potential) of all molecules.
(b) For a monoatomic gas, the number of degrees of freedom is 3.
(c) v_rms > ⟨v⟩ > v_p for any ideal gas.
(d) Dalton's law of partial pressures applies to any mixture of gases.
(e) The mean free path increases when the number density increases.
(f) At the same temperature and pressure, equal volumes of all ideal gases contain the same number of molecules.
(g) Cₚ > Cᵥ for all ideal gases; Cₚ − Cᵥ = R.
(h) Each vibrational mode of a molecule contributes ½k_BT to its average energy.
(i) The ideal gas equation is PV = μRT, where T must be in Kelvin.
(j) Carbon (diamond) obeys the Dulong-Petit law at room temperature.

---

### ✅ D ANSWERS

**Q71:**
(a) P = (1/3)nmv̄²
(b) ½mv̄² = (3/2)k_BT
(c) v_rms = √(3k_BT/m)
(d) R (= 8.31 J mol⁻¹ K⁻¹)
(e) l = 1/(√2 π n d²)
(f) f = 5; Cᵥ = 5R/2; γ = 7/5
(g) C ≈ 3R ≈ 24.9 J mol⁻¹ K⁻¹
(h) P_i = μ_i RT/V = n_i k_B T
(i) lesser (v_rms ∝ 1/√m; heavier → lower v_rms)
(j) E = (3/2)μRT = (3/2)Nk_BT

**Q72:**
(a) **False** — Temperature measures average *translational* KE per molecule = (3/2)k_BT. The total energy includes rotational and vibrational contributions too.
(b) **True** — Monoatomic: 3 translational DOF (no rotation, no vibration at ordinary T).
(c) **True** — Always: v_rms = √3, ⟨v⟩ = √(8/π), v_p = √2 (in units of √(k_BT/m)); 1.73 > 1.60 > 1.41.
(d) **False** — Dalton's law applies to mixtures of *non-reacting (non-interacting)* ideal gases only.
(e) **False** — Mean free path *decreases* when number density increases (l ∝ 1/n).
(f) **True** — This is Avogadro's hypothesis, derivable from PV = Nk_BT.
(g) **True** — Mayer's relation: Cₚ − Cᵥ = R for any ideal gas.
(h) **False** — Each vibrational mode contributes **k_BT** (not ½k_BT) because it has two quadratic energy terms (kinetic + potential energy).
(i) **True** — Temperature T must always be in Kelvin (absolute temperature scale).
(j) **False** — Carbon (diamond) is the *main exception* to Dulong-Petit at room temperature; its molar heat capacity is only ~6.1 J/mol·K (far below 3R ≈ 24.9 J/mol·K).

### ✅ E ANSWERS

**Q73.**
v_rms = √(3RT/M₀) = √(3 × 8.31 × 300 / 28 × 10⁻³)
= √(7479 / 0.028) = √(267,107) = **517 m/s ≈ 516 m/s**

**Q74.**
l = 1/(√2 π n d²) = 1/(1.414 × 3.14159 × 2.7×10²⁵ × (3×10⁻¹⁰)²)
= 1/(1.414 × 3.14159 × 2.7×10²⁵ × 9×10⁻²⁰)
= 1/(1.414 × 3.14159 × 2.43×10⁶)
= 1/(1.079 × 10⁷) ≈ **9.3 × 10⁻⁸ m ≈ 0.93 × 10⁻⁷ m**

**Q75.**
He is monoatomic (Cᵥ = 3R/2), Ar is monoatomic (Cᵥ = 3R/2).
U_total = μ₁(3/2)RT + μ₂(3/2)RT = (1 + 1) × (3/2) × 8.31 × 300
= 2 × 12.465 × 300 = 2 × 3739.5 = **7479 J ≈ 7.5 kJ**

**Q76.**
He is monoatomic: Cᵥ = 3R/2. Cylinder is at constant volume.
ΔQ = μCᵥΔT = 2 × (3/2 × 8.31) × 20 = 2 × 12.465 × 20 = **498.6 J ≈ 499 J**

**Q77.**
v_rms = √(3RT/M₀) → v_rms² = 3RT/M₀
T = v_rms² × M₀ / (3R) = (1200)² × 4×10⁻³ / (3 × 8.31)
= 1440000 × 0.004 / 24.93 = 5760 / 24.93 ≈ **231 K**

**Q78.**
ΔQ = μCᵥΔT → ΔT = ΔQ/(μCᵥ) (same μ for both — equal volumes at same T and P → same n → same N → same moles)
ΔT_A = Q/(μ × 3R/2) = 2Q/(3μR)
ΔT_B = Q/(μ × 5R/2) = 2Q/(5μR)
**ΔT_A/ΔT_B = (5R)/(3R) = 5/3 ≈ 1.67**
(Monoatomic gas rises to higher temperature for the same heat input — smaller Cᵥ)

**Q79.**
Cᵥ = 29.1 J/mol·K ≈ 7R/2 (since 7 × 8.31/2 = 29.1 J/mol·K)
This corresponds to f = 7 → **diatomic gas with one vibrational mode active (high temperature)**
γ = Cₚ/Cᵥ = (9R/2)/(7R/2) = **9/7 ≈ 1.286**

**Q80.**
Collision frequency Z = ⟨v⟩/l = 493 / (2.9 × 10⁻⁷)
= **1.7 × 10⁹ collisions per second**
(A nitrogen molecule undergoes ~1.7 billion collisions per second at STP!)

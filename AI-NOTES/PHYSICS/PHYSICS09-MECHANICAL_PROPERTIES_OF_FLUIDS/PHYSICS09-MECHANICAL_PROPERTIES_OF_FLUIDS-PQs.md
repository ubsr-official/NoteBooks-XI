**Q41.** 🟢
**Assertion (A):** Blood pressure in humans is greater at the feet than at the brain.
**Reason (R):** Pressure in a fluid increases with depth (height of fluid column above).

---

**Q42.** 🟢
**Assertion (A):** A hydraulic lift is based on Pascal's law.
**Reason (R):** Pressure applied to an enclosed fluid at rest is transmitted undiminished in all directions.

---

**Q43.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** A spinning cricket ball in air does not follow a parabolic trajectory.
**Reason (R):** Due to the Magnus effect, spinning creates unequal pressure on two sides of the ball, resulting in a lateral force.

---

**Q44.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** In a horizontal pipe of non-uniform cross-section, the liquid flows faster in the narrower region.
**Reason (R):** At the narrower region, the pressure is lower.

---

**Q45.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** Two soap bubbles of different sizes, when connected by a tube, the smaller bubble shrinks and the larger bubble grows.
**Reason (R):** Excess pressure inside a soap bubble is inversely proportional to its radius; so smaller bubble has higher pressure and air flows to the larger bubble.

---

**Q46.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** Viscosity of gases increases with temperature, while viscosity of liquids decreases with temperature.
**Reason (R):** In liquids, increasing temperature disrupts intermolecular bonds making layers flow more easily. In gases, increasing temperature increases intermolecular collisions, increasing momentum transfer between layers.

---

**Q47.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** Water can rise in a capillary tube to a greater height than mercury can be depressed in the same tube.
**Reason (R):** Surface tension of water is greater than surface tension of mercury.

---

**Q48.** 🔴
**Assertion (A):** Bernoulli's equation cannot be used to describe the flow of water through a rapid in a river.
**Reason (R):** Flow through a river rapid is turbulent, and Bernoulli's equation applies only to steady (non-turbulent) flow.

---

**Q49.** 🔴
**Assertion (A):** Terminal velocity of a small raindrop is proportional to the square of its radius.
**Reason (R):** Stokes' law gives the drag force as F = 6πηav, and at terminal velocity, the drag + buoyancy = gravitational force.

---

**Q50.** 🔴
**Assertion (A):** The pressure inside a soap bubble is greater than atmospheric pressure.
**Reason (R):** The surface tension of the soap film creates an inward pressure, compressing the air inside.

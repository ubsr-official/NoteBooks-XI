**Q42.** 🟢
**Assertion (A):** Work done by friction is always negative.
**Reason (R):** Friction always opposes motion, so the angle between friction force and displacement is always 180°.

* (a) (b) (c) (d)

---

**Q43.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** A body moving with uniform speed in a circular path has zero net work done by the centripetal force.
**Reason (R):** The centripetal force is always perpendicular to the instantaneous velocity of the body.

* (a) (b) (c) (d)

---

**Q44.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** In a perfectly elastic collision between two equal masses (one at rest), the first body comes to rest and the second moves with the initial speed of the first.
**Reason (R):** In elastic collisions between equal masses, momentum and kinetic energy are simultaneously conserved, giving a unique solution.

* (a) (b) (c) (d)

---

**Q45.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** The potential energy of a spring is the same whether compressed or extended by the same amount x.
**Reason (R):** PE of a spring = ½kx², which depends on x² (always positive), not on the sign of x.

* (a) (b) (c) (d)

---

**Q46.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** A satellite moving in a circular orbit does not require any work to maintain its orbit.
**Reason (R):** In circular orbit, gravity provides centripetal force. Work by gravity = 0 (force ⊥ displacement).

* (a) (b) (c) (d)

---

**Q47.** 🔴
**Assertion (A):** During an elastic collision, kinetic energy is not conserved at every instant of the collision.
**Reason (R):** During contact, bodies deform and some kinetic energy is temporarily stored as elastic potential energy of the deformed region.

* (a) (b) (c) (d)

---

**Q48.** 🔴
**Assertion (A):** Total mechanical energy of a system can be increased even by non-conservative forces.
**Reason (R):** Work done by non-conservative forces changes the total mechanical energy by ΔE = Wnc, which can be positive (e.g., motor doing work).

* (a) (b) (c) (d)

---

**Q49.** 🔴
**Assertion (A):** Two bodies have the same kinetic energy but different masses. The one with larger mass has greater momentum.
**Reason (R):** p = √(2mK); at constant K, p increases with m.

* (a) (b) (c) (d)

---

**Q50.** 🟡
**Assertion (A):** The work done by the net force equals change in kinetic energy even if the path is curved.
**Reason (R):** The work-energy theorem, derived from Newton's second law, is valid for any path (not just straight line), as work is calculated as the line integral of force along the actual path.

* (a) (b) (c) (d)

**Q71.** 🟢 A force of 50 N acts on a body at 30° to the displacement of 6 m. Calculate work done.

**Answer:**
W = Fd cosθ = 50 × 6 × cos30° = 300 × (√3/2) = **150√3 ≈ 259.8 J**

**Q72.** 🟢 A body of mass 5 kg is moving at 6 m s⁻¹. A force brings it to rest in 2 m. Find the force.

**Answer:**
K_i = ½×5×36 = 90 J; K_f = 0
By WE theorem: W = K_f − K_i = −90 J
W = F × d × cos180° → −F × 2 = −90 → **F = 45 N**

**Q73.** 🟢 Calculate the power of a pump that lifts 200 kg of water per second to a height of 5 m. (g = 10 m s⁻²)

**Answer:**
Work done per second = mgh/t = 200 × 10 × 5 = **P = 10,000 W = 10 kW**

**Q74.** 🟡 A block of mass 2 kg slides down a frictionless inclined plane of height 3 m. Find (a) PE at top, (b) KE at bottom, (c) speed at bottom.

**Answer:**
(a) PE = mgh = 2×10×3 = **60 J**
(b) KE = 60 J (all PE converts to KE, no friction)
(c) ½mv² = 60 → v² = 60 → v = **√60 = 7.75 m s⁻¹**

**Q75.** 🟡 A 3 kg body moving at 4 m s⁻¹ collides head-on elastically with a 1 kg body moving at 2 m s⁻¹ in the opposite direction. Find their velocities after collision.

**Answer:**
m₁ = 3 kg, v₁ᵢ = +4 m s⁻¹; m₂ = 1 kg, v₂ᵢ = −2 m s⁻¹

Treating system with v₂ᵢ = −2 m s⁻¹, using elastic collision formulas:
Let u₁ = 4, u₂ = −2

$$v_1 = \frac{m_1-m_2}{m_1+m_2}u_1 + \frac{2m_2}{m_1+m_2}u_2 = \frac{2}{4}(4) + \frac{2}{4}(-2) = 2 - 1 = \mathbf{1 \text{ m s}^{-1}}$$

$$v_2 = \frac{2m_1}{m_1+m_2}u_1 + \frac{m_2-m_1}{m_1+m_2}u_2 = \frac{6}{4}(4) + \frac{-2}{4}(-2) = 6 + 1 = \mathbf{7 \text{ m s}^{-1}}$$

Both move in +x direction after collision.

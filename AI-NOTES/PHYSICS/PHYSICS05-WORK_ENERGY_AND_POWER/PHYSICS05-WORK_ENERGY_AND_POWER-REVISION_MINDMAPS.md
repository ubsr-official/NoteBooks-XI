## 🔢 Key Definitions — Absolute Must-Memorise

| Quantity | Definition | Formula | SI Unit |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Work** | Product of force and displacement in direction of force | W = F·d = Fd cosθ | J (Joule) |
| **Kinetic Energy** | Energy due to motion | K = ½mv² = p²/2m | J |
| **Gravitational PE** | Energy stored due to position at height h | V = mgh | J |
| **Spring PE** | Energy stored in a deformed spring | V = ½kx² | J |
| **Mechanical Energy** | Sum of KE and PE | E = K + V | J |
| **Power** | Rate of doing work | P = dW/dt = F·v | W (Watt) |
| **Impulse** | Change in momentum (Ch. 4 link) | J = Δp = FΔt | N s |
| **Spring Constant** | Stiffness of spring; F = −kx | k = F/x | N m⁻¹ |

## 📊 Important Comparisons — Instant Recall

```
CONSERVATIVE vs NON-CONSERVATIVE FORCES:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Conservative:    Work path-independent; ΔV = −W; PE defined
                 Examples: Gravity, spring, electrostatic
Non-conservative: Work path-dependent; PE NOT defined
                 Examples: Friction, viscosity, air drag
                 With friction: Ef − Ei = Wnc < 0

ELASTIC vs INELASTIC COLLISION:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Both:            Momentum conserved (always)
Elastic:         KE conserved; e = 1; no deformation
Inelastic:       KE not conserved; e < 1
Perf. Inelastic: Maximum KE lost; bodies stick; e = 0
                 KE lost = ½[m₁m₂/(m₁+m₂)]v₁ᵢ²

WORK vs POWER vs ENERGY:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Work:     Force × displacement (scalar, can be negative)
Energy:   Capacity to do work (scalar, can be potential/kinetic)
Power:    Rate of doing work (scalar, always > 0 for positive work)
1 kWh = energy (power × time), NOT a unit of power
```

## ⚠️ Critical Distinctions — High-Yield Traps

```
WORK TRAPS:
  ✓ Work by gravity on upward motion = −mgh (negative)
  ✓ Work by normal force on horizontal motion = 0 (⊥)
  ✓ Porter walking with load on head: W by porter on load = 0
  ✓ W₁₂ + W₂₁ ≠ 0 in general (unlike force pairs)
  ✓ Work done depends on displacement, NOT distance (vectors)
  ✓ For circular motion: W by centripetal force = 0 always

ENERGY TRAPS:
  ✓ KE is always positive (½mv² ≥ 0)
  ✓ PE can be negative (if reference is not at lowest point)
  ✓ "Satellite speeds up as it spirals in despite losing total E"
     → PE decreases more than KE increases; net E decreases
     → Speed increases because r decreases (orbital mechanics)
  ✓ V = ½kx²: symmetric — same PE for compression/extension

COLLISION TRAPS:
  ✓ Momentum: ALWAYS conserved in collision
  ✓ KE: conserved ONLY in elastic (not in general)
  ✓ Equal mass elastic: first stops, second gets all velocity
  ✓ During collision, even elastic: KE ≠ conserved (deformation)
  ✓ Coefficient of restitution e = 1 (elastic); 0 (perf. inelastic)
  ✓ 2D elastic equal masses: final velocities are ⊥ to each other

POWER TRAPS:
  ✓ kWh is energy (unit), not power
  ✓ 1 hp = 746 W (not 550 W — that's ft·lb/s definition)
  ✓ P = F·v uses instantaneous velocity
  ✓ At maximum speed (terminal): P = resistive force × vmax
```

## 🔁 Energy Method vs WE Theorem — Decision Chart

```
WHICH APPROACH TO USE?
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Step 1: Check if ALL forces are conservative

  ALL conservative? → Use Conservation of Mechanical Energy
                       Ki + Vi = Kf + Vf
                       (simplest, no work calculations needed)

  Friction / air drag / motor present?
                    → Use Work-Energy Theorem:
                       Kf − Ki = Wnet
                     OR Modified:
                       Ef − Ei = Wnc
                       (account for friction work separately)

Examples:
  Ball rolling down frictionless ramp → K + V = const
  Car skidding to stop → KE lost = friction force × distance
  Car colliding with spring (with friction) → WE theorem
  Pendulum bob (no friction) → K + V = const
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

## 🏆 Last-Minute Exam Checklist

```
Before answering any Work, Energy, Power problem:

□ Is displacement zero? (If yes, W = 0 regardless of force)
□ What is the angle θ between F and d? (0°, 90°, or 180°?)
□ Can I use K+V = const? (Only if no friction/non-conservative forces)
□ If friction present: use WE theorem; Wnc = −f × displacement
□ For spring problems: V = ½kx² (always +ve); F = −kx (restoring)
□ For collision: identify elastic or inelastic first
□ Perfectly inelastic: use vf = m₁v₁/(m₁+m₂); NEVER use KE conservation
□ Equal mass elastic: first body STOPS; complete transfer
□ Is power asked? P = W/t or P = Fv (use instantaneous velocity)
□ kWh = energy unit = 3.6 × 10⁶ J (not power!)
□ For loop-the-loop: v₀ = √(5gL) at bottom; vC = √(gL) at top
□ Potential energy zero is arbitrary — choose convenient reference
□ Check sign of work: negative work → energy taken from body
```
